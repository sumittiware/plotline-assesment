# Incentives & Offers

Incentives — credits, discounts, trials, unlocked features — can tip a hesitant user into action, but they're easy to overuse and can erode margin and train users to wait for deals. Use them deliberately.

## When an incentive makes sense

- **Win-back of churned users**, where the alternative is losing them entirely.
- **Removing a specific friction** (e.g., a free trial of a paid tier for an engaged free user who's hitting limits).
- **Time-bounded activation** for new users who stalled before reaching value.

## When to avoid

- For users who would have acted anyway (active, already-converting users) — you're just giving away margin.
- As a substitute for a real value proposition. If the only reason to act is the discount, the campaign has a messaging problem.
- Repeatedly to the same users, which teaches them to wait for the next offer.

## Framing

- Lead with the **value or outcome**, not the discount. "Unlock advanced segments for your next campaign" beats "20% off!"
- Make terms and expiry clear and honest. Hidden conditions destroy trust.
- Keep urgency real. A countdown that resets is a trust violation users notice.

## Targeting

Match incentive size to the user's value and the difficulty of the action. A long-churned enterprise user may warrant a meaningful offer; an active free user trying one more feature needs none. Always check that the user is eligible for what you're offering (plan, region) before promising it.
