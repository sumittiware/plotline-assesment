# Frequency Capping & Message Fatigue

Every message spends a little of the user's goodwill. Send too many and users disengage, mute notifications, unsubscribe, or uninstall — and once that permission is gone, it rarely comes back. Frequency discipline protects the long-term ability to reach users at all.

## Caps

- Limit the number of campaign messages a user receives per week. A small number is the norm; err on the side of fewer.
- Apply caps **per channel** and **overall** — three pushes, two emails, and four in-app prompts in a week is fatigue even if no single channel feels heavy.
- Transactional and critical messages (security, account) are generally exempt from marketing caps, but shouldn't be abused as a loophole for promotion.

## Suppression rules

- Suppress a user from a campaign if they recently received a similar one.
- Suppress users who have already taken the campaign's desired action — don't ask someone to try a feature they just tried.
- Honor a cooldown after any message before the next campaign can target the same user.

## Signs of fatigue to watch

- Rising opt-out / unsubscribe rates.
- Falling open and response rates across campaigns.
- Increasing notification disablement or uninstalls following sends.

If these climb, reduce frequency before tweaking copy — volume is usually the cause.

## Prioritization

When multiple campaigns could target the same user in the same window, pick the single most relevant/valuable one rather than sending all of them. A prioritization rule (most relevant wins, others wait or drop) is healthier than letting campaigns stack.
