# Win-back & Churned Users

Churned users (no activity in 30+ days) are a different problem from lapsed users. They've stopped finding value, may have forgotten what the product does, and likely won't see in-app messages. Win-back is about giving them a fresh, compelling reason to return — not a reminder that they left.

## Principles

- **Assume amnesia.** Re-introduce the core value as if they're new, but acknowledge their prior relationship lightly.
- **Lead with what's changed.** New features, improvements, or fixes since they left are your strongest hook — especially if tied to why they may have churned.
- **Reach them off-app.** Email is usually the only viable channel; push works only if still enabled. In-app is pointless for users who aren't opening the app.
- **Make returning frictionless.** One click back to value, not a re-onboarding maze.

## Incentives

A targeted incentive (a credit, a trial of a paid tier, an unlocked feature) can be appropriate for win-back where it would be wasteful for active users. Use sparingly and make the value, not the discount, the headline.

## Cadence and restraint

- A short, finite series (2–3 messages over a few weeks) outperforms an indefinite drip.
- If there's no response, stop. Repeatedly emailing churned users drives spam complaints and hurts deliverability for your whole list.
- Honor unsubscribes immediately and completely.

## Measuring

Win-back success is a *return* (a real session or core action), not an open or a click. Judge campaigns on reactivation, and watch for unsubscribe and complaint rates as guardrail metrics.
