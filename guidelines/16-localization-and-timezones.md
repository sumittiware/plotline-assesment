# Localization & Timezones

Our users span many countries, and a message that ignores their language, context, or local time lands worse than no message at all.

## Timezones

- Send relative to the user's **local** time, derived from their country/region — not a single global send time.
- Respect local quiet hours (roughly 9pm–8am local). A message timed for mid-morning in one region is the middle of the night in another.
- For global campaigns, schedule per-timezone waves rather than one blast, so everyone receives it at a sensible local hour.

## Language and tone

- Match the language to the user's locale where supported. A message in a language the user doesn't read is wasted and feels careless.
- Localization is more than translation: idioms, examples, currency, and date formats should fit the locale. Keep currency and amounts consistent with the user's region.
- Keep the brand voice consistent across languages — helpful, concise, human — even as the wording adapts.

## Content and cultural fit

- Avoid idioms, slang, or cultural references that don't translate.
- Be mindful of local holidays and norms; a promotion tied to one region's calendar may be irrelevant or insensitive elsewhere.
- Keep copy simple enough to localize cleanly — overly clever wordplay rarely survives translation.

## Practical defaults

When locale data is missing or ambiguous, fall back to a safe default (a neutral language and a conservative mid-day send window in the user's best-known region) rather than guessing aggressively. Better a slightly generic message at a reasonable hour than a perfectly tailored one at 3am.
