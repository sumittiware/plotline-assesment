# A/B Testing & Experimentation

Don't ship messaging changes on intuition. When a campaign matters, test it — and measure against the outcome you actually care about, not a proxy.

## What to test

- **Copy:** subject lines, push titles, the framing of the value proposition.
- **Channel and timing:** does push or in-app drive more returns for this segment?
- **Incentive vs. no incentive**, or different incentive sizes.
- **Segment definition:** a tighter vs. broader audience.

Test one variable at a time so you can attribute the difference. Changing copy, channel, and timing at once tells you nothing about which mattered.

## Designing the test

- Split the target segment randomly into comparable groups.
- Keep a holdout/control where feasible — "no message" is a legitimate and often illuminating variant.
- Decide the success metric *before* launching, and make it a real outcome (return, feature adoption, conversion), not an open or click.
- Run long enough and on a large enough audience to tell signal from noise; tiny samples produce confident nonsense.

## Reading results

- A higher open or click rate that doesn't move the real outcome is not a win.
- Watch guardrail metrics (unsubscribes, opt-outs, complaints) alongside the primary metric — a variant that lifts clicks but spikes opt-outs is a loss.
- Don't stop a test the moment it looks good; early leads frequently reverse.

## Rolling out

Once a variant clearly wins on the outcome metric, roll it out — ideally gradually, watching that the result holds on the full population before making it the default.
