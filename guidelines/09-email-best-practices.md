# Email Best Practices

Email is our most tolerant channel for length and detail, and often the only way to reach users who aren't opening the app. But inbox competition is fierce, so the subject line and first impression do most of the work.

## Subject lines

- Keep them under ~50 characters; many clients truncate beyond that, and mobile previews are shorter still.
- Be specific and benefit-led. "Your 3 unread campaign reports" beats "Newsletter #42."
- Avoid spam triggers: ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation, "FREE!!!", misleading "Re:" / "Fwd:" prefixes.
- The subject must match the body. Bait-and-switch subjects tank long-term open rates and trust.

## Preheader

The preview text after the subject is prime real estate. Use it to extend the subject's promise, not to repeat it. Never leave it to auto-fill with "View in browser…".

## Body

- One primary call to action, visually prominent. Secondary links are fine but shouldn't compete.
- Front-load the value; assume users skim and many never scroll.
- Keep it scannable: short paragraphs, clear hierarchy.
- Always include a working unsubscribe link — it's both legally required in most regions and better for list health than burying it.

## Deliverability

- Sending to unengaged or churned users at high volume hurts your sender reputation; prune or slow down for cold segments.
- Watch complaint and bounce rates as guardrails.

## When to use email vs. other channels

Prefer email for longer-form value, win-back of churned users, and anything the user might want to revisit later. For time-sensitive nudges to active users, in-app or push will reach them faster.
