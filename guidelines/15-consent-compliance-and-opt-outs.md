# Consent, Compliance & Opt-outs

Messaging permission is a privilege governed by user choice and, in many regions, by law. Respecting it isn't just compliance — it's what keeps your channels deliverable and your brand trusted.

## Consent

- Only message users on channels they've consented to. Push requires the user to have enabled notifications; email marketing requires opt-in in many jurisdictions.
- Treat channel-level consent separately: a user who accepts in-app messages has not necessarily agreed to email or push.
- Never message users who have opted out of a category, even if they're a perfect fit for the segment.

## Opt-outs and unsubscribes

- Every marketing email must include a clear, working unsubscribe link.
- Honor opt-outs immediately and completely across the relevant channel — a user who unsubscribes should not receive the next scheduled send.
- Make opting out easy. Hiding or obstructing it drives spam complaints, which damage deliverability far more than the lost unsubscribe.

## Data and personalization limits

- Personalize only with data you legitimately have and that the user would expect you to use.
- Never expose sensitive information or make the user feel surveilled. "Because you used X" is fine; anything that feels invasive is not.

## Suppression lists

Maintain and respect global suppression lists (unsubscribed, bounced, complained). These override any campaign targeting — a user on the suppression list is never a valid target, regardless of how well they match a segment.

## Regional differences

Rules vary by region (consent, timing, content disclosures). When a campaign spans countries, apply the stricter standard rather than the looser one.
