# Measuring Campaign Success

A campaign isn't successful because it was sent or opened — it's successful because it moved the outcome it was designed to move. Define that outcome before launch and judge against it.

## Pick the right metric per goal

- **Re-engagement / win-back:** a real *return* — a session or core action after the message — not an open or click.
- **Onboarding:** activation (reaching the first meaningful action) and early retention.
- **Feature adoption:** the user actually using the targeted feature.
- **Conversion campaigns:** the upgrade/purchase, not the click that preceded it.

Opens and clicks are *diagnostic* signals (did the subject/copy work?), not success metrics. A campaign can have great open rates and zero impact.

## Guardrail metrics

Always watch the downside alongside the upside:

- Unsubscribe and opt-out rates.
- Spam complaints and notification disablement.
- Uninstalls following sends.

A campaign that lifts its primary metric while spiking these is a net loss.

## Attribution and honesty

- Use a holdout where possible — comparing targeted users to a comparable untargeted group is the only way to know the campaign *caused* the lift rather than coinciding with it.
- Beware crediting the campaign for actions users would have taken anyway.

## Closing the loop

Feed results back into segmentation and copy. A campaign that consistently underperforms on its real outcome should be reworked or retired, not re-sent. Track results over time, not just per-send, to catch fatigue and diminishing returns.
