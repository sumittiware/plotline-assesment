# Feature-Adoption & Education Campaigns

These campaigns drive existing, active users to try a feature they haven't used yet. They're high-value because the audience is already engaged — you're deepening usage, not reviving it.

## Finding the audience

The target is the gap between what a user *could* use and what they *have* used:

- Take the full feature catalog (`features`).
- Subtract the features the user has triggered `feature_used` events for.
- The remainder is their set of unadopted features.

Prioritize features that are (a) relevant to the user's existing behavior and (b) high-value or "sticky" — features correlated with retention are the best ones to push.

## Messaging

- Explain the feature in terms of the user's goal, not its mechanics. "Turn your survey responses into a segment in one click" beats "Introducing Segments."
- Show the payoff up front. Why should they spend 30 seconds learning this?
- Link directly into the feature, pre-filled with their context where possible.

## What to avoid

- Pushing a feature the user already uses heavily (check adoption first).
- Promoting features irrelevant to the user's plan or platform (don't pitch a paid feature to someone who can't access it without saying so).
- Bundling several features into one message — pick the single most relevant one.

## Sequencing

Introduce one feature at a time, spaced out. A user who adopts one new feature is a good candidate for the next, so feature-adoption campaigns work well as an ongoing, behavior-triggered series rather than a one-off blast.
