# In-App Messaging

In-app messages reach users while they're already engaged, which makes them the highest-converting and least intrusive channel — but only for users who are actually opening the app. They're useless for lapsed or churned users.

## When to use

- Contextual nudges tied to what the user is doing right now.
- Feature education for active users.
- Announcements relevant to the current screen or task.
- Surveys and feedback prompts at natural moments.

## Format guidance

- **Banners / inline:** low interruption, good for non-urgent tips. The user can ignore them.
- **Modals:** higher interruption, reserve for genuinely important or high-value prompts. Overusing modals trains users to dismiss reflexively.
- **Tooltips / coachmarks:** good for in-context feature discovery.

Match the intrusiveness of the format to the importance of the message. A minor tip does not deserve a full-screen modal.

## Timing within a session

- Don't interrupt a user mid-task with an unrelated message. Wait for a natural pause (task complete, returning to a home screen).
- Never stack multiple in-app messages in one session; show the single most relevant one and hold the rest.

## Copy

- Short, contextual, and actionable. The user's attention is already on the screen, so get to the point.
- Provide a clear, easy dismiss. A message the user can't easily close generates frustration and bad-faith taps.

## Frequency

Even though in-app is low-cost, it isn't free attention. Cap how often a given user sees prompts per session and per week, and suppress messages they've already acted on or dismissed.
